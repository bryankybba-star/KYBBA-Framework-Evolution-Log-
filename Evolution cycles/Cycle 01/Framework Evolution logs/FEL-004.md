FEL-004

Title

Discovery of Interdependence as the Dominant Architectural Relationship

---

Evolution Cycle

Evolution Cycle 1

---

Stage

Structural Roles

---

Background

During the evaluation of the Structural Roles of each Entity Type Category, the framework sought to understand how the categories were organized within a Location.

Initially, it was expected that the categories would naturally form a hierarchical structure.

---

Problem

The framework required an architectural explanation describing how Entity Type Categories relate to one another within a functioning Location.

---

Discovery

Analysis showed that no single Entity Type Category could independently realize a complete Location.

Instead, each category relied on the capacities of other categories, forming an interconnected system of mutual dependencies.

---

Decision

The framework recognizes interdependence as the dominant architectural relationship between Entity Type Categories.

Hierarchical relationships may still exist in specific contexts, but the foundational architectural relationship is one of structural interdependence.

---

Framework Impact

This discovery established the foundation for the next stage of development: Entity Dependency Architecture.

It also shifted the framework away from explaining Locations as hierarchical collections of components toward understanding them as integrated systems.
