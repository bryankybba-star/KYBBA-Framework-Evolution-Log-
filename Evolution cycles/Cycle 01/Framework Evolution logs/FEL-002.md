FEL-002

Title

Structural Roles Adopted as the Validation Method for Entity Type Categories

---

Evolution Cycle

Evolution Cycle 1

---

Stage

Structural Roles

---

Background

As Structural Roles were being defined, questions emerged regarding whether certain Entity Type Categories should remain independent or be merged because of apparent similarities.

Examples included:

- Capital and Resources
- Resources and Infrastructure Assets

---

Problem

Classifying Entity Type Categories based solely on physical similarity could produce an inconsistent architectural model.

---

Decision

Entity Type Categories would be validated according to their Structural Roles rather than their physical characteristics or apparent similarities.

---

Reasoning

A category is justified by the unique structural contribution it makes to the framework, not by what it looks like or what it contains.

Structural Roles therefore became the architectural basis for validating Entity Type Categories.

---

Framework Impact

The framework established Structural Validation as the primary method for confirming Entity Type Categories.

This approach strengthened the architectural consistency of the framework and reduced ambiguity during future development.
