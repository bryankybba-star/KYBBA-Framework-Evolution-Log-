Purpose

This document records architectural principles that emerged during Evolution Cycle 1 but require validation before becoming confirmed Design Principles.

---

CP-001 — Interdependence as the Dominant Architectural Relationship

Entity Type Categories primarily relate through structural interdependence rather than strict hierarchy.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-002 — System Capacities Form the Bridge Between Structure and Function

System Capacities provide the architectural connection between Entity Type Categories and higher-level system functions.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-003 — Structural Roles Predict Future Dependencies

Once Structural Roles are correctly defined, future Entity Dependencies should emerge naturally from them.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-004 — Architectural Complexity Should Emerge Progressively

Framework complexity should increase only after lower architectural layers have been completed and stabilized.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-005 — Stable Architecture Minimizes Future Redesign

Completing architectural foundations before introducing detailed components reduces future restructuring.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-006 — Discovery-Driven Development Produces More Robust Frameworks

Allowing framework architecture to emerge through structured discovery may produce stronger long-term architectural consistency than predetermined design.

Status: Pending Validation

---

CP-007 — Every Structural Layer Should Produce New Architectural Knowledge

Each completed architectural layer should contribute new insights, principles, or relationships rather than simply expanding existing definitions.

Status: Pending Validation

---

Validation Policy

Candidate Principles remain provisional until they are tested and supported through subsequent Evolution Cycles.

Once sufficient architectural evidence has been gathered, a Validation History (VH) record will be created, and the principle may be promoted to a confirmed framework principle.
