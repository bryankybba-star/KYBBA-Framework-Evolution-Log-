Validation History

Repository: KYBBA Framework Evolution Log

Path: Validation-History/README.md

---

Purpose

The Validation History directory preserves the official record of how Candidate Principles become Confirmed Principles within the KYBBA Framework.

While Evolution Cycles document discoveries and Framework Evolution Logs (FELs) record significant architectural decisions, Validation History documents the evidence that confirms or refines those discoveries over time.

This directory ensures that every permanent framework principle can be traced back to its origin, evaluated through subsequent Evolution Cycles, and verified using documented architectural evidence.

---

Scope

Validation History records include:

- Candidate Principles proposed during Evolution Cycles.
- Evolution Cycles in which validation occurred.
- Supporting architectural evidence.
- Refinements made during validation.
- Final validation outcome.
- Date of confirmation within the framework's evolution.

---

Validation Process

A principle follows this progression:

Architectural Discovery

↓

Candidate Principle

↓

Evaluation Across Future Evolution Cycles

↓

Validation

↓

Confirmed Principle

↓

Framework Version

Validation is based on documented architectural evidence rather than assumption or opinion.

---

Validation Record Structure

Each Validation History entry will include:

- Validation History Identifier (VH-XXX)
- Principle Title
- Originating Evolution Cycle
- Supporting Framework Evolution Logs (FELs)
- Validation Evidence
- Validation Decision
- Framework Impact
- Final Status

---

Current Status

Therefore, no Validation History entries have been created.

The first Validation History record (VH-001) will be created when the first Candidate Principle is successfully confirmed through future Evolution Cycles.

Guiding Principle

Validation within the KYBBA Framework is an ongoing architectural process.

Principles are not considered permanent because they appear reasonable. They become permanent only after repeated evaluation, supporting evidence, and successful validation across the framework's continued evolution.
