Glossary

Purpose

This glossary defines the terminology used throughout the KYBBA Framework Evolution Log. It establishes a common vocabulary for documenting the framework's development and ensures that all Evolution Cycles, Framework Evolution Log (FEL) entries, Validation History records, and Framework Versions use consistent terminology.

---

Architectural Insight

An understanding or realization about the architecture of the framework that emerges during development.

Architectural Insights explain what was discovered about the framework but are not themselves design rules.

---

Candidate Principle

A proposed framework principle that is supported by current architectural evidence but has not yet been fully validated.

Candidate Principles remain hypotheses until confirmed through future Evolution Cycles.

---

Confirmed Principle

A Candidate Principle that has been successfully validated through subsequent framework development and is recognized as a permanent framework principle.

---

Design Principle

A rule that guides how the framework should be designed and developed.

Design Principles define the methodology of framework construction rather than the behavior of the framework itself.

---

Emergent Pattern

A recurring behavior, relationship, or architectural structure that repeatedly appears during framework development without being intentionally designed.

Emergent Patterns often provide evidence for future Candidate Principles.

---

Evolution Cycle

A completed phase of framework development focused on a specific architectural or design objective.

Each Evolution Cycle concludes with documented discoveries, decisions, principles, summaries, and Framework Evolution Log entries before progressing to the next stage.

---

Framework Evolution Log (FEL)

A chronological engineering record documenting significant architectural questions, alternatives considered, decisions made, discoveries, reasoning, impacts, and validation status throughout the development of the KYBBA Framework.

Each entry is assigned a unique identifier (e.g., FEL-001).

---

Framework Version

A stable and validated release of the KYBBA Framework representing the successful completion of multiple Evolution Cycles.

Framework Versions are created only after sufficient architectural maturity has been achieved.

---

Major Discovery

A significant finding that substantially improves the understanding, structure, methodology, or architecture of the framework.

Major Discoveries frequently lead to Architectural Insights, Design Principles, Emergent Patterns, or Candidate Principles.

---

Structural Validation

The process of evaluating a framework component through consistent architectural reasoning rather than intuition or assumption.

Structural Validation provides objective justification for architectural decisions.

---

System Capacity

The unique structural capability contributed by an Entity Type Category that enables higher-level system functions within the framework.

System Capacities bridge the Design and Architecture stages of the framework.

---

Validation History (VH)

A chronological record documenting the validation of Candidate Principles into Confirmed Principles.

Each Validation History entry is assigned a unique identifier (e.g., VH-001).

---

Validation Status

The current level of confidence assigned to a discovery, architectural decision, or framework principle.

Validation Status may include:

- Proposed
- Under Evaluation
- Requires Future Validation
- Confirmed
- Refined
- Superseded

---

Version Milestone

A major point in framework development where multiple Evolution Cycles have been completed, validated, and consolidated into an official Framework Version.

---

Guiding Philosophy

The KYBBA Framework is developed through structured discovery rather than predetermined assumptions.

Every significant architectural decision should be traceable, reviewable, evidence-based, and open to refinement as future Evolution Cycles provide additional validation.
